# Quick Start Guide

## 60 Seconds to Your First Strategy

### 1. Install Dependencies
```bash
cd D:\mvp
npm install
```

### 2. Start Development Server
```bash
npm run dev
```

Visit `http://localhost:3000` in your browser.

### 3. Fill Out the Form
- **Community Name:** Your Skool community's name
- **Niche:** What's your community about? (e.g., "Copywriting", "Product Development")
- **Description:** What problems does your community solve? How is it different?
- **Target Audience:** Who should join? Be specific. (e.g., "Solo founders with $10-100k ARR")

### 4. Click "Generate Strategy"
The AI analyzes your inputs and creates:
- 8-10 search keywords
- Short 10-15 word tagline
- 100-150 word About page copy
- 7 ready-to-publish posts for launch week

### 5. Copy & Paste
Each section has a copy button. Paste directly into:
- Skool community About page
- Skool discussion posts
- Your landing page or social media

## What You'll Get

### Keywords
Search terms people use to find communities like yours:
```
• "copywriting community"
• "learn copywriting with other writers"
• "copywriting group coaching"
• etc.
```

### Short Description (for banners/taglines)
```
"Learn copywriting from people actually getting paid to write."
```

### Long Description (for About page)
```
This community is for freelance writers and content creators who want to 
level up their skills and earn more. We focus on practical copywriting 
techniques, real client work breakdowns, and peer feedback. 

Here's how it works: each week we analyze a real-world copy example, 
break down what works, and discuss why. You'll also get feedback on your 
own work from people who understand the craft...
```

### 7-Day Content Plan

**Day 1 - Welcome Post**
Welcome message introducing the community and what members get

**Day 2 - Value/Tip**
One actionable tip they can implement today

**Day 3 - Engagement Question**
Question to spark discussion and learn about your audience

**Day 4 - Testimonial/Social Proof**
Share a member win (or your own)

**Day 5 - Poll**
Quick poll to understand audience needs

**Day 6 - Behind-the-Scenes**
Share your actual process or system

**Day 7 - Call-to-Action**
Encourage members to take one specific action

## Tips for Best Results

**Be specific with your inputs:**
- ❌ "Marketing" → ✅ "High-ticket B2B sales for SaaS founders"
- ❌ "Everyone" → ✅ "Solo founders with 0-$10k MRR looking to hire their first employee"
- ❌ "Cool community" → ✅ "Peer group focused on accountability and [specific outcome]"

**Describe what makes you different:**
- ❌ "Community for writers" → ✅ "Community for technical writers in DevTools, focused on helping them grow indie consulting practices"
- ❌ "We help people succeed" → ✅ "We break down real sales calls and analyze what worked—with people who understand B2B sales"

**Be your target audience:**
- Think like your ideal member
- What would make them want to join?
- What outcome are they actually after?

## Iteration Tips

1. **Generate multiple versions** - Try different descriptions of your niche/audience, see what resonates
2. **Test keywords** - Use them in your About page, see what drives discovery
3. **Track post performance** - Notice which post types get engagement, emphasize those
4. **Get feedback** - Share your strategy with 2-3 potential members, ask if they'd join
5. **Refine and regenerate** - After Month 1, regenerate with feedback from your early members

## Next Steps After Generation

1. **Update your Skool community:**
   - Copy About page description
   - Update community title/tagline if needed
   - Add a banner with your short description

2. **Plan your launch:**
   - Pick a start date
   - Schedule the 7-day posts in advance (if Skool supports it) or plan to post daily
   - Invite your first 20-30 members directly

3. **Prepare content:**
   - Edit posts if needed (all are editable after copying)
   - Add personal touches
   - Add images or resources where relevant

4. **Track what works:**
   - Note which posts get replies
   - Which questions spark conversation?
   - What topics would members like more of?

5. **Plan Month 2:**
   - Use Month 1 learnings to adjust your strategy
   - Regenerate a new 30-day content plan if needed
   - Double down on what worked

## Troubleshooting

**I'm getting generic content**
- Make your inputs more specific (describe exactly who you serve and why they need your community)
- Describe what makes your approach different

**I don't like the keywords**
- These are what your audience searches for—focus on whether they match your positioning, not whether you like them

**The posts don't sound like me**
- All content is editable after copying! Make it yours. The goal is to save you starting from scratch, not to be perfect as-is.

**Can I use this for multiple communities?**
- Yes! Generate a strategy for each community. Each niche/audience needs different positioning.
